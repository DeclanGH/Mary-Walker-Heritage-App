Oswego City Hall and W. Oneida St

Dr. Mary Walker was not afraid to speak her mind to politicians, including in an audience with Abraham Lincoln. Perhaps that is why she was often filmed candidly in the area of City Hall. She was considered an oddity when she walked the streets in pants, coat and top hat, and people would take surreptitious photos of her. Even though Walker was a Medal of Honor recipient and Civil War veteran, people often insulted her. Some written accounts say people even threw tomatoes at her as she walked through town. She was once arrested (not in Oswego) for “impersonating a man.” When asked why she wore men’s clothes, she responded: “I don’t wear men’s clothes; I wear my own clothes.”
